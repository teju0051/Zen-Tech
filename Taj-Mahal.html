Taj Mahal, Delhi

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most iconic monuments in the world. Built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, it serves as a stunning symbol of love and loss, dedicated to his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Constructed entirely of white marble, the Taj Mahal is renowned for its intricate carvings, inlaid gemstones, and symmetry. The monument’s beauty is especially mesmerizing at sunrise and sunset, when the changing light plays on the marble, making it appear to change color. Inside, the main mausoleum houses the tombs of Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal. Surrounding the Taj Mahal are beautifully manicured gardens, a reflecting pool, and a mosque, all contributing to its serene and majestic atmosphere. A UNESCO World Heritage site, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors each year, offering a glimpse into India's rich history and architectural brilliance. When visiting, tourists are encouraged to take their time exploring the grounds, learn about its fascinating history, and enjoy the breathtaking views from various vantage points. Don’t forget to capture the iconic shot of the Taj Mahal reflecting in the pool, a memory that will last a lifetime!
